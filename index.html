Pilot briefing

HOW TO PLAY

  1. Suit up. Enter a callsign, then create a new game or join with a friend’s room code. The host starts the challenge and can pause, resume, or abandon an active round.
  2. Read the mission HUD. Every round a scripted attacker messes with the network. Your objective stays at the top: keep block times stable, or keep any one algo below its share limit.
  3. Work the sliders. The 4 sliders are your mining rigs across the 4 proof-of-work algos. Moving hash toward or away from a lane pushes its difficulty up or down via the LWMA.
  4. Read the battlefield. It’s tower defense: army color identifies the algorithm, black armor with a warning horn marks attackers, and white helmet diamonds mark your squad. The closer attackers get to the chain tower, the more compromised that lane is. Each lane’s wall is its difficulty; a rising wall (and TIP-004 freezes) drives invaders back. Watch for orphans (two blocks found at once — one crumbles) and shadow miners stacking ghost blocks to rewrite the chain.
  5. Or let the Copilot fly. Autopilot can play the round for you — it explains every move in its log and learns from past rounds.
  6. You’re doing science. Each finished round records a datapoint comparing the status-quo network config vs the proposed one — see the research panel at the bottom.

HOW YOU WIN

Each challenge has one scored network objective on the mission card and top HUD: a stability threshold, algo-share limit, or maximum reorg depth.

The room wins or loses together based only on that objective at round end. Result stats marked SCORED decide the verdict; mean block time, orphans, longest wait, difficulty swing, and other health stats are research context unless marked.

Personal points and MVP are separate: mined blocks earn points, consecutive wins add streak bonuses, and reorged-away blocks can have their points clawed back. They never override the network verdict.
